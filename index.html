Student Progression at Odisee

A systems-thinking approach to understanding study efficiency, dropout, and progression gaps in the Brussels context.

The Wicked Challenge

Our wicked challenge is improving academic success at Odisee through systemic, programme-level change. We focus on student progression indicators such as study efficiency and dropout rates, and examine how these are shaped by the interaction between student characteristics and the educational environment in Brussels.

The Brussels Learning Environment

Image source: Odisee website

Odisee’s Brussels campus is characterised by diversity, multilingualism, and varied student living and commuting realities. Understanding how programme structures, teaching practices, assessment, and student support interact within this context is essential for explaining progression gaps and identifying leverage points for change.
