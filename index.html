Student Progression at Odisee

A systems-thinking approach to understanding study efficiency, dropout, and progression gaps at programme level in the Brussels context.

Image source: Brik / Odisee page

The Wicked Challenge

Our wicked challenge is improving academic success at Odisee through systemic, programme-level change. In this project, we focus on student progression indicators (e.g., study efficiency and dropout) and examine how they are shaped by the interaction between student characteristics and the educational environment in Brussels.

The Brussels Learning Environment

Image source: Odisee website

Odisee’s Brussels campus context is characterised by diversity, multilingualism, and different student living and commuting realities. Understanding how programme structures, teaching and assessment practices, and student support interact within this context is essential for explaining progression gaps and identifying leverage points for change.
